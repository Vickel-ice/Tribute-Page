Nelson Mandela

South Africa's first black head of state

1918 - 2013

He emerged from decades of imprisonment not with a desire for vengeance, but with a message of forgiveness and reconciliation. A moral compass for the world, his leadership showed that true strength lies in unity and compassion. He will forever be remembered as a symbol of hope and the father of a free South Africa.

Achievements

  • He led the fight to end apartheid, which was a system of strict racial separation in South Africa.
  • After spending 27 years in prison, he was released and became a symbol of hope and peace.
  • He became the first Black president of South Africa in 1994, in the country's first truly democratic election.
  • He won the Nobel Peace Prize for his role in ending apartheid without a civil war.
  • He created the Truth and Reconciliation Commission to help the country heal from the past.
